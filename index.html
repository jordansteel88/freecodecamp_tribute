Bob Ross

Timeline

  • 1942 - Bob Ross was born in Daytona Beach, Florida on October 29th.
  • 1961 - Enlisted for the US Air Force at the age of 18.
  • 1965 - Bob Ross married his first wife Vivian Ridge.
  • 1977 - Married his wife Jane Ross. Their marriage lasted until her death in 1992.
  • 1981 - Bob Ross adopts his curly hair to save money on haircuts.
  • 1983 - The Joy of Painting's first season was produced by WNVC in Falls Church, Virginia.
  • 1984 - The show moved to WIPB in Muncie, Indiana and was broadcast to a national audience.
  • 1985 - The Joy of Painting began using guest appearances from other painters beginning in Season 3, who paint alongside Bob.
  • 1986 - The Martin F. Weber Company introduced a line of Bob Ross painting products, and the 3 hour Workshop video was released.
  • 1989 - Bob Ross's first hardcover book, The Best of the Joy of Painting, was released.
  • 1991 - The Joy of Painting's 300th episode aired in the United States, and then in Canada. Soon, The Joy of Painting began broadcasting internationally in many translations and satellites around the world, such as Mexico, Costa Rica, Colombia, the United Kingdom, Europe, Latin America, the Netherlands, Greece, Germany, Switzerland, Austria, Turkey, Iran, South Korea, Australia, and Japan.
  • 1992 - The Joy of Painting began syndicating to PBS stations across the country under the moniker The Best of the Joy of Painting; and his second hardcover book, More Joy of Painting, was released. Sadly, Bob Ross's wife Jane Ross died of cancer that same year.
  • 1994 - The Joy of Painting was cancelled by PBS stations across the country, and Bob Ross returned back to his hometown Orlando, Florida. Bob Ross appeared on Bill Nye the Science Guy (season 1, episode 20: "Eyeball") in a self-parody segment entitled "The Artistic Eye with Bob Ross". Bob Ross made his final talk show appearance on The Phil Donahue Show.
  • 1995 - Bob Ross teamed up with a crew from Muncie, to produce a show called The Adventures of Elmer and Friends. But he was too ill to travel to Indiana to shoot the pilot. So the crew came along, and recorded Bob's parts from his home. Bob Ross died from lymphoma on July 4th at the age of 52.
